Plant Growth Experiment

Objective

The objective of the plant growth experiment is to determine how different light conditions affect the growth of plants. This involves: Investigating Growth Variability: Observing how plants perform under various lighting situations (full sunlight, partial sunlight, and no sunlight). Understanding Light Requirements: Gaining insights into the importance of light as a critical factor in plant development, which can include aspects such as height, leaf health, and overall vitality. Learning Scientific Methodology: Engaging in the scientific method by formulating a hypothesis, conducting controlled experiments, collecting and analyzing data, and drawing conclusions based on observations.

Materials Needed

  • 3 identical pots
  • 3 small plants (e.g., bean plants)
  • Soil
  • Ruler
  • Water
  • Light source (lamp, sunlight, etc.)
  • Notebook for observations

Instructions

  1. Fill each pot with the same amount of soil.
  2. Plant one seedling in each pot.
  3. Label each pot according to the light condition it will receive:
    • Pot 1: Full sunlight
    • Pot 2: Partial sunlight
    • Pot 3: No sunlight (place in a dark area)
  4. Water each plant equally every 2 days.
  5. Place the pots in their designated light conditions.
  6. Measure the height of each plant weekly using a ruler.
  7. Document your observations and any other notable changes in growth.
  8. Continue the experiment for at least 4 weeks.

    9. Results

    After 1 week (2 weeks total) observe if “leached” cups now have plants that are growing. Did leaching help the same for saline vs. alkali soils?

    After 2 weeks, measure the height of plants in each cup and record the results. Again, observe the roots and record observations on the data sheet.
